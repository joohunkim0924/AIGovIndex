How it works

Methodology

Each jurisdiction is positioned based on how strongly its AI governance framework pulls toward each of three philosophical poles. Two views available: a ternary triangle (three-pole) and a two-axis compass (individual rights vs state control / voluntary vs binding).

01
Three governance philosophies
Rights-protective, market-oriented, and sovereignty-centered. Ternary coordinates sum to 1.0. No country is purely one model.
02
Two views
Triangle view shows three-way tension simultaneously. Compass view shows individual rights vs state control (vertical) against voluntary vs binding law (horizontal) — more intuitive for general audiences.
03
Dot size = enforcement
Dot size independently encodes binding enforcement strength. Larger = stronger legally enforceable mechanisms with meaningful penalties.
04
Timeline 2016–2026
Drag the year slider to see how governance frameworks evolved over a decade — from the ethics era through binding enforcement.
Key insight: EU and China are both large dots (strong binding enforcement) but at opposite corners. Binding enforcement and rights protection are independent dimensions — China uses binding law to enforce state control, the EU uses it to protect individual rights.
