How it works

Methodology

Each jurisdiction is positioned on a ternary plot — a triangular coordinate system — based on how strongly its AI governance framework pulls toward each of three philosophical poles.

01
Three governance philosophies
Every framework is evaluated across three dimensions: rights-protective orientation, market-oriented flexibility, and state sovereignty emphasis. Coordinates sum to 1.0.
02
Dot size = enforcement
Dot size independently encodes binding enforcement strength — whether the framework has legally enforceable obligations with meaningful penalties. Larger = stronger binding law.
03
Position ≠ ranking
This is a positioning tool, not a grading system. No country scores "higher" than another. The map reveals philosophical orientation and governance architecture, not quality.
Key insight: The EU and China are both large dots (strong binding enforcement) but sit at opposite corners of the triangle. This reveals that binding enforcement is not the same as rights protection — China uses binding law to enforce state control, the EU uses it to protect individual rights. Enforcement strength and governance philosophy are independent dimensions.
