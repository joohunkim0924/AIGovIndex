How it works

Methodology

Each jurisdiction is positioned from the same underlying data in two complementary views: a ternary Triangle and a two-axis Compass. Dot size independently encodes binding enforcement strength in both views.

01
Three governance philosophies
Rights-protective, market-oriented, and sovereignty-centered. These three poles form the analytical foundation for both visualizations. No country is purely one model.
02
Triangle view
Plots each country on a ternary diagram with coordinates r, m, and s (rights, market, sovereignty) that sum to 1.0. Best for seeing all three philosophical pulls at once — e.g. how market-oriented vs. sovereignty-centered a framework is.
03
Compass view
Re-projects the same data onto two axes: vertical rx (individual rights vs. state control) and horizontal bx (voluntary soft law vs. binding hard law). The four quadrants show combinations like “rights-protective binding law” or “state control soft law.”
04
Dot size = enforcement
In both views, dot size encodes binding enforcement strength. Larger dots mean stronger legally enforceable mechanisms with meaningful penalties — independent of where a country sits on either axis.
05
Timeline 2016–2026
Drag the year slider to see how positions shift over time in either view. Crisis milestones appear at key years from 2022 onward.
Key insight: On the Triangle, the EU and China sit at opposite corners; on the Compass, they land in opposite vertical halves (rights vs. state control) while both lean rightward toward binding law. Enforcement strength and philosophical orientation are independent dimensions.
